HISTORY OF SAREES


The saree is the traditional dress of women in the Indian subcontinent. The word saree is derived from the Sanskrit word “sareeka” which means “long piece of cloth”.


The saree has a history that dates back more than 3000 years ago. It was originally worn by Hindu women in ancient India and has been worn by many other civilizations since then. There are many different types of traditional sarees in India, but they all have a few things in common - they are long pieces of fabric that are wrapped around the body, they can be draped over one shoulder or both shoulders and can be held together on one side with a belt or pin.
